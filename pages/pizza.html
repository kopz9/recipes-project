Pizza Recipe

Description

Mozzarella pizza is amazing. Even more so, with pasta and homemade tomato sauce.

Add some basil and invite the whole family to the table.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups (tea) of wheat flour (about 840 g)
  • 2 tablespoons of instant dry yeast (about 20 g)
  • 2 teaspoons of sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 2½ cups (tea) of warm water
  • ¼ cup (tea) olive oil
  • wheat flour to sprinkle the countertop
  • olive oil to grease the bowl

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix the yeast and sugar. Add warm water and, using a spoon, mix well to dissolve. Leave at room temperature until foamy - about 5 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, in the bowl of a stand mixer, mix the flour with the salt and make a well in the center. Set aside a bowl or other large container and grease it with 2 tablespoons of olive oil - it has to have enough space for the dough to rise a lot.
  3. Once it foams, pour the yeast mixture into the center of the bowl with flour. Add ¼ cup (tea) of olive oil and mix, with a spatula, just to combine.
  4. To knead the dough: fit the bowl onto the mixer with the hook; start beating at low speed for 5 minutes; gradually increase the speed to high and let it knead for another 15 minutes until a smooth dough forms - it will come away from the side of the bowl and form a ball on the mixer hook.
  5. Using your fingertips (or a spatula), loosen the dough from the mixer bowl and transfer it to the container greased with olive oil. Cover with film and let rest for 1 hour, until it rises and doubles in volume.
  6. When there are 30 minutes left to complete the dough's rising time, preheat the oven to 250 ºC (high temperature). If you are using a soapstone baking tray, place it in the oven while it is still cold to heat it from the beginning - otherwise it could crack due to the thermal shock.
